What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is a type of bullying and or harassment that is typically done on electronic devices and is more commonly known as online bullying. Cyberbullying more than likely will occur on social media such as Instagram snapchat etc. But can also happen on email, online forums, and even online games. It is also significantly more common among teenagers.

Heres is the definition according to stopbulling.gov Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. Cyberbullying can occur through SMS, Text, and apps, or online in social media, forums, or gaming where people can view, participate in, or share content. Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation. Some cyberbullying crosses the line into unlawful or criminal behavior.
